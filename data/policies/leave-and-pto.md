# Leave & Paid Time Off Policy

## Overview
This policy explains the types of leave available to full-time employees, how
much leave you are entitled to each calendar year, and how to apply. The leave
year runs from 1 January to 31 December. All figures below are for full-time
employees; pro-rata entitlements apply to part-time staff.

## Leave Types and Annual Entitlement
Full-time employees receive three categories of paid leave each year:

- **Casual Leave — 12 days per year.** For short, planned personal matters and
  errands. Casual leave cannot be combined with earned leave for a single
  absence of more than five consecutive working days.
- **Sick Leave — 12 days per year.** For personal illness or medical
  appointments. A medical certificate is required for any sickness absence of
  three or more consecutive days.
- **Earned Leave (also called Privilege Leave) — 18 days per year.** For
  planned holidays and longer breaks. Earned leave should be requested at least
  two weeks in advance where possible.

## Accrual and Carry-Forward
Casual and sick leave are credited in full at the start of the leave year and
do not carry forward — any unused balance lapses on 31 December. Earned leave
accrues monthly and up to 30 days may be carried forward into the next year.

## How to Apply
Apply for leave through the HR Concierge or the Keka self-service portal by
selecting the leave type, the number of days, and the start date. Your current
balance is shown before you submit. Applications are validated against your
available balance; you cannot apply for more days than you have.

## Approval and Notice
Casual and sick leave are auto-approved subject to balance. Earned leave of
more than five consecutive days requires manager approval. Sick leave may be
applied for retrospectively where prior notice was not possible.

## Public Holidays
Public holidays are listed separately in the annual holiday calendar and do not
count against any leave balance.

## Leave Encashment
On resignation, unused earned leave is encashed at basic salary. Casual and
sick leave are not encashable.

## Unpaid Leave
Where paid balances are exhausted, unpaid leave may be granted at management
discretion and must be agreed with your manager in advance.
